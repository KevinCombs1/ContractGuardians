Frequently Asked Questions

When is a contract void or unenforceable?

Contracts are void if illegal or against public policy. They are voidable if signed under fraud, duress, misrepresentation, or undue influence. Courts evaluate enforceability based on statutory and common law.

Can I exit a contract just because I changed my mind?

No. Valid grounds for exit include fraud, unconscionable terms, misrepresentation, or violation of statutory protections. Exiting without legal grounds can result in penalties.

Do you provide legal representation or advice only?

We partner with licensed attorneys for full legal representation when necessary. Our team provides guidance, handles negotiations, and ensures full compliance with legal standards.
