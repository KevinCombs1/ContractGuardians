Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a contract legally void or unenforceable?

Contracts may be void if they involve illegal subject matter or violate public policy. A contract is voidable if signed under fraud, misrepresentation, duress, undue influence, or mistake. Courts will consider enforceability based on statutory and common law principles.

Can I exit a contract just because I changed my mind?

No. Exiting a contract without legal grounds usually triggers penalties. Valid grounds include fraud, misrepresentation, unconscionable terms, or statutory consumer protections.

Do you provide legal representation or only advice?

Contract Guardians partners with licensed attorneys to provide full legal representation when necessary. We educate clients and ensure compliance, then handle negotiations or court filings if required.
